About.

Electrical engineering Student University at Buffalo:

Hello I’m Saul, currently a senior attending the University at Buffalo, I enjoy studying electrical engineering. Nearing the end of my undergraduate career I have taken a great interest in the power sector, more specifically power electronics. I've grown to have a passion for power electronics because of the applications that revolve around the subject. For example, in the automotive industry power electronics is used to create more environmentally friendly cars. My interest in automotive and power electronics led me to take on various design projects. One design project that I worked on was, a DC motor that varied the speed of the attached axil. The DC motor design functioned off a FPGA that changed the duty cycle for the attached H-Bridge circuit that used the DC motor as a load. Another design project that I was a part of was the design tor a traction drive system for a hybrid car. There are two critical circuit topologies used to make up the tractions drive system. Using power simulation software both the, back-to-back DC converter, and gate drive circuits where analyzed to fit the cost and the thermal resistance of a cooling plate for a particular IGBT. Aside from my time dedicated to engineering I enjoy devolving other skills as well. Some extra curricular that I enjoy are playing guitar, partaking in various physical activities such as soccer swimming, and jujitsu. In fact, I have dedicated enough time to jujitsu that in 2012 I competed, and came in first place in the battle at the beach tournament in Wildwood New Jersey. Furthermore, I am fluent in two languages, which are Spanish and English.
